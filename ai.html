What is Artificial Intelligence?

Artificial Intelligence (AI) refers to the simulation of human intelligence processes by computer systems. These processes include learning (acquiring information and rules), reasoning (using rules to reach approximate or definite conclusions), and self-correction. AI has evolved from simple rule-based systems to complex neural networks capable of perception, natural language understanding and autonomous decision-making.

Key insight: Modern AI systems do not "think" like humans — they identify statistical patterns in vast datasets. Yet the outcomes can be remarkably human-like, from generating coherent text to diagnosing cancer from medical scans.

Core Branches of AI

  • 🧠Machine Learning – algorithms that improve automatically through experience without being explicitly programmed.
  • 🔬Deep Learning – multi-layer neural networks that learn representations from raw data (images, text, audio).
  • 💬Natural Language Processing – enabling computers to understand, generate and translate human language.
  • 👁️Computer Vision – processing and interpreting visual information from the world, powering face recognition and self-driving cars.
  • 🤖Robotics & Autonomy – physical systems that perceive their environment and act upon it intelligently.
  • 🎮Reinforcement Learning – training agents to make decisions by rewarding desirable behaviour.

A Brief History of AI

1950

Turing Test Proposed

Alan Turing publishes "Computing Machinery and Intelligence," introducing the concept of a machine that can exhibit intelligent behaviour.

1956

Dartmouth Conference

The term "Artificial Intelligence" is coined. The field is born as a formal academic discipline.

1997

Deep Blue Defeats Kasparov

IBM's chess-playing computer defeats world chess champion Garry Kasparov, a landmark in AI capability.

2012

AlexNet & Deep Learning Revolution

A deep convolutional neural network dramatically outperforms all competitors on ImageNet, sparking the modern deep learning era.

2022

ChatGPT & Generative AI Boom

OpenAI releases ChatGPT, bringing large language models to 100 million users in under two months — the fastest adoption of any consumer technology.

AI Applications Today

AI is now deeply embedded in everyday life, often invisibly. Here are some prominent application areas:

  • Healthcare: AI assists in diagnosing diseases from medical images, drug discovery, and personalising treatment plans.
  • Finance: Fraud detection, algorithmic trading, and credit risk assessment all rely heavily on machine learning.
  • Education: Adaptive learning platforms personalise coursework to individual student progress.
  • Transportation: Semi-autonomous vehicles use AI for perception, prediction and path planning.
  • Creative Industries: Generative models produce text, images, music and code at scale.
