Looking Ahead

Future Technology Trends

Quantum computing, biotechnology, brain-computer interfaces and the metaverse — the technologies that will define the next decade.

Quantum Computing

Quantum computers exploit the principles of quantum mechanics — superposition and entanglement — to perform certain calculations exponentially faster than classical computers. While current quantum processors remain error-prone and operate at temperatures colder than outer space, rapid progress suggests that fault-tolerant quantum systems will emerge in the 2030s.

Potential applications include breaking (and creating) cryptographic systems, accelerating drug discovery by simulating molecular behaviour, and optimising complex logistics networks.

Quantum Supremacy: In 2019, Google claimed its 53-qubit Sycamore processor performed a specific calculation in 200 seconds that would take the best classical supercomputer approximately 10,000 years.

Biotechnology & Gene Editing

CRISPR-Cas9 gene editing technology allows scientists to precisely cut, delete or replace sections of DNA with unprecedented accuracy. Since its development in the early 2010s, CRISPR has moved rapidly from laboratory research into clinical trials, offering hope for treating genetic diseases such as sickle cell anaemia, certain cancers and inherited blindness.

Beyond medicine, synthetic biology is engineering microorganisms to produce sustainable fuels, biodegradable plastics and novel materials.

Brain-Computer Interfaces

Brain-computer interfaces (BCIs) create a direct communication pathway between the brain and external devices. Neuralink (founded by Elon Musk) and other companies are developing implantable chips capable of reading neural signals and translating them into computer commands. Potential applications include restoring mobility to paralysed patients, treating neurological disorders and, further ahead, augmenting human cognition.

The Road Ahead

  • ⚛️Quantum Networks – unhackable communication channels using quantum key distribution.
  • 🧬Personalised Medicine – treatments tailored to an individual's genome for maximum efficacy and minimal side effects.
  • 🌱Green Technologies – next-generation solar cells, solid-state batteries and green hydrogen production.
  • 🥽Spatial Computing – seamless blending of digital and physical environments through AR/VR headsets and holographic displays.
  • 🤝Human-AI Collaboration – AI systems that amplify human creativity, decision-making and scientific discovery.
  • ♻️Circular Economy Tech – AI-driven waste sorting, chemical recycling and biodegradable material design.
